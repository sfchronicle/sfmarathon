A strong majority of runners are 29, but average pace is relatively constant across ages
The bars on the chart show the distribution of ages of SF marathon registered runners from 2010 through 2016. The superimposed line shows the average pace of all the runners of each age in 2016.

About the data: We are only including average paces for ages that include at least 50 runners in 2016 to retain accuracy.
Average pace corresponds to the hill profile
Most runners are at the mercy of the hills, the average pace varying by almost up to two minutes depending on the grade. Everyone gets tired at the end too, even though there are no hills.

About the data: Strava gave us this data, because Strava is awesome. Average paces are based on data from Strava users who uploaded their race data to the site, and pace is averaged on a per mile basis.
Runners spread out and slow down on the hills
The fastest runners have a more consistent pace than other runners. As the race progresses, the runners spread out as some slow down more than others.
About the data: Strava gave us this data, because Strava is awesome. Data is plotted for Strava users who uploaded their race data to the site.
